1.  THE CORE IDENTITY CONCEPT
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

The Private Suite OS is built on a single governing idea: this app is the
diegetic device that grants access to ROA's world. It is not a fan portal, a
merch shop, or a concert app tacked onto a streaming profile. It is the
interface of a private room — one you were either invited into or earned your
way into. Every design decision flows from that premise.

ROA's artistic identity operates in a specific emotional register: romantic but
dangerous, intimate but exclusive, warm but nocturnal. The design system
translates that register into a UI language with the same duality. Surfaces are
cold. Accents are warm. The darkness is not generic — it is a specific room at
2am, in a specific city, with a specific energy.

The artist's nickname "El Lobo" and the fan community language "La Manada"
(the pack) provide the social identity backbone of the app. Users are not
followers or subscribers — they are pack members with rank, progression, and
status. This is embedded in every section from the Ranking leaderboard to the
Quest system on the Profile page.


────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
2.  PERSONALITY & EMOTIONAL REGISTER
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

The app has five distinct personality traits, each expressed across the design
system with deliberate consistency.

NOCTURNAL
─────────
The app lives at night. Not as an aesthetic choice, but as a structural one.
Every background, surface, and interaction pattern is calibrated for low-light,
high-contrast environments. Page backgrounds never reach pure black (#080C18
Abyss, #101628 Void) because pure black reads as empty rather than deep. The
darkness here has color temperature: the global system is cool-dark (Estate
Blue, Navy Blazer), the store is warm-dark (Malt Void, Chocolate Malt), and the
landing page is purple-dark (Black Bean, Total Eclipse). Three different rooms,
all nighttime.

INTIMATE BUT INACCESSIBLE
──────────────────────────
The glassmorphism treatment is not decorative. Glass surfaces use Estate Blue at
6% opacity as a tint, with a 1px top specular highlight at 8% Cloud Dancer
opacity — this creates a sense that the card is a window into something behind
it, not just a flat container. The content is visible but separated. You can see
the room through the glass; you are not yet in it. This maps directly to the
Private Suite metaphor: the app is the keyhole before the door opens.

EARNED, NOT DECORATIVE
────────────────────────
Color in this system is semantic, not aesthetic. Fort Knox gold (#9A8838) does
not appear as a brand color, a hover state, or a decorative border. It appears
exclusively on completed quests, achievement badges, and the level indicator dot
on the user avatar — because those are the only contexts where something has
been earned. Warm Platinum (#C8C4BE) marks identity (the avatar glow) while
gold marks achievement. The distinction is intentional and non-negotiable: if
gold decorates, it stops signaling completion. Similarly, Lava Falls (#A32830)
is limited to one element per full-page view on the landing. If it appears
twice, it stops being a scream and becomes wallpaper.

PACK ENERGY — SOCIAL, RANKED, WOLFISH
───────────────────────────────────────
The app is explicitly a community product, not a passive content destination.
The Ranking section frames song and album votes as "pack status." Quest
completion language uses phrases like "La Manada." Rank badges progress through
a wolf-themed hierarchy. This is not cosmetic branding layered on top of a
generic app — it is the organizing logic of the social features. The Ranking
leaderboard uses cold precision (Anthracite, Lavender Blue, Crystal Gray, Metal)
because it is a championship display, not a playlist. The Community section
softens deliberately (Peach Whip, Lavender Fog, Ethereal Blue) because packs
also rest.

CINEMATIC RESTRAINT
────────────────────
The visual references for this system — the BLOODY album cover, the NOON cover,
Berserk imagery — are maximalist in scale but minimalist in color. Type is
enormous. Palettes are ruthlessly narrow. The landing hero follows this logic:
one massive Bebas Neue headline, one Lava Falls word, one horizontal Ancient
Water light streak, one halftone grain overlay. The restraint is what gives each
element its impact. A system that uses seven accent colors has no accent colors.
This system uses one per section, deployed once.


────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
3.  THE SEASONAL COLOR ARCHITECTURE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

The system is organized around three color seasons, each governing a distinct
zone of the app. The seasons represent emotional registers that coexist in the
same interface, each appropriate to a different kind of interaction.

  WINTER  ·  Landing, Auth, Checkout, Forum
            High contrast, glass-ice restraint, editorial sleekness.
            Key colors: Estate Blue, Navy Blazer, Teal Green, Cloud Dancer.

  AUTUMN  ·  Store / Drops, Events, Ranking
            Ember and crimson spikes, rich warmth, pack energy, contained danger.
            Key colors: Persian Red, Ember Malt, Chocolate Malt, Amberlight, Rabbit.

  SUMMER  ·  Community Chat, Forum, Profile
            Muted, opaque glass, readable, calm belonging, intimacy without urgency.
            Key colors: Peach Whip, Lavender Fog, Slightly Pink, Ethereal Blue.

4.  TYPOGRAPHY SYSTEM
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

The type system uses four fonts in strict roles. No font crosses into another's
territory. Each font is a different register of voice, not a different weight of
the same voice.

BEBAS NEUE — THE VOICE
───────────────────────
Used exclusively for display text: hero headlines, section titles, rank
positions, city names in events, countdown numerals, drop names, and quest
reward values. Heavy, condensed, zero-apology. One weight, one style — uppercase
only. This is not a limitation; it is the rule. At 96–200px on the landing hero,
it bleeds through the artist figure, becoming structure rather than label.

BARLOW CONDENSED — THE SYSTEM VOICE
─────────────────────────────────────
Weight 600–700. Handles all UI infrastructure: navigation items, badge labels,
section headers, column headers, metadata labels, and button text. Always
uppercase, always tracked at 0.15–0.3em. This is the font users read without
noticing — the one that says STORE, LIMITED, TONIGHT, SOLD OUT, VIP PRESALE,
ENTER SUITE. It is the system talking, not the brand performing.

BARLOW — THE HUMAN VOICE
─────────────────────────
Weight 300–600. Handles all prose, body content, product descriptions, forum
posts, chat messages, and quest descriptions. The only font in the system that
appears at mixed case. In the Community section it runs at 15–16px — deliberately
larger than elsewhere — because legibility in conversation is a courtesy to the
user that the brand does not interrupt.

SPACE MONO — THE DATA VOICE
─────────────────────────────
Handles all numeric and technical data: hex codes, prices, countdowns,
statistics, timestamps, and rank change indicators. Always monospaced and always
tabular — every digit occupies identical width so prices and rankings align in
columns. Critical in the Store (prices must be read at a glance) and Ranking
(numerical column alignment is part of the scoreboard aesthetic).


────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
5.  THE GLOBAL ACCENT SYSTEM
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Six accent colors run across the entire system. Each owns a specific semantic
role. None of them decorate — they signal. If you see a color, you know what it
means. If a color appears in a context where its meaning does not apply, it is
being misused.

  #007A78  Teal Green
           Glassmorphism tint, all interactive states, hover/focus, In Stock
           badge, On Sale badge. Omnipresent — the ambient signature of the system.

  #A32830  Lava Falls
           ONE typographic punctuation element per full page view on landing.
           Limited stock badge in store. Rare and deliberate — maximum one use
           per view.

  #D06020  Ember Malt
           Drop countdowns, Tonight event badge, live queue pulse, streak
           indicators. Urgency and heat moments only — never decorative.

  #9A8838  Fort Knox
           Quest completion, achievement badges, level indicator dot, exclusive
           reward coupons. Achievement-only — if it decorates, it loses meaning.

  #7C3AED  Royal Violet
           VIP / Presale ticket badge, exclusive profile reward, rare rank signal.
           Rarest accent — exclusivity language only.

  #C8C4BE  Warm Platinum
           Avatar border, avatar glow, avatar initials — presence and identity.
           Identity signal only — never achievement, never navigation.

6.  SECTION-BY-SECTION IDENTITY
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

6.1  LANDING / HERO  ·  Season: Winter — Editorial
───────────────────────────────────────────────────
The landing page is a cinematic editorial entry point, not a marketing homepage.
The design reference is the BLOODY and NOON album covers — massive type bleeding
through a dark figure, halftone grain, a single warm color punctuating a cold
field. Black Bean (#231F24) is the stage floor. Total Eclipse (#24283C) panels
are the wings. Ancient Water (#C8D2E0) is the cold display type — all hero
headlines are this color. Lava Falls (#A32830) appears on exactly one word.

The hero contains a background type layer — the word ROA rendered at 3–5% opacity
behind the primary headline, creating depth without competing with it. A horizontal
Ancient Water gradient line cuts across the frame at 38% height, referencing a
studio monitor or a spotlight beam. Corner metadata (PRIVATE SUITE OS, EST. 2022 ·
NARANJITO, PR) sits in Barlow Condensed at near-invisible opacity, grounding the
image in specificity without demanding attention.

The navigation bar uses near-black with high backdrop blur and Barlow Condensed 700
for all items. The active item uses Teal Green with a 1px bottom border — not a
background fill, not a pill. The CTA buttons in the hero are the only place where
Lava Falls appears as a filled button background, and only the primary CTA gets it.

6.2  STORE / DROPS  ·  Season: Autumn — Warm Dark
──────────────────────────────────────────────────
The store is the section where heat enters the system. Malt Void (#14100C) is
the page background and unified card surface — warm dark, almost amber-black.
Persian Red (#A02030) is the scarcity signal. Ember Malt (#D06020) drives
countdowns. Chocolate Malt provides warm editorial display context.

All active product cards (Limited, In Stock, Presale) share Malt Void as their
background, keeping the surface consistent so badges do all the differentiation.
The sold-out card uses Navy Blazer (#1A2038) — a deliberate temperature shift
from warm to cold. The switch reads as "closed" before the user reads the badge
or sees the desaturation overlay. The desaturation filter applies on top of Navy
Blazer, making sold-out cards both cooler and colorless against the warm active
grid.

Every active card has a 1px Amberlight (#D8B898) specular top border at 20%
opacity — the chrome edge of a display case. The Virtual Queue panel uses Persian
Red tint with a live progress bar showing units remaining, transitioning from
Persian Red to Ember Malt as inventory depletes. The Checkout summary card uses
the global glassmorphism recipe — Estate Blue tint, backdrop blur, specular top
border.

The Ranking section is governed by cold precision. Anthracite (#282A2A) and

6.5  COMMUNITY (CHAT + FORUM)  ·  Season: Summer — Still
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The Community section is the Summer zone — the only place in the app where the
tension deliberately drops. This is not a lower-priority section. It is a section
with a different emotional job: keeping users present, comfortable, and talking.
Urgency would work against that goal.

Glass panels here are more opaque than in the rest of the app — backdrop-filter
blur is reduced and fill opacity is increased to 80% (vs 60% system-wide). This
creates a sense of walls rather than windows. Own message bubbles use a Lavender
Fog (#C5BFD0) tint at 12% opacity. Other users' messages use Peach Whip
(#D2ADA0) at 8%. The color temperature of conversation is warm on both sides —
mutual, not directional.

Ethereal Blue (#58A8CA) is the sole interactive color in this section — links,
@mentions, reply buttons. No Lava Falls, no Ember Malt, no countdown energy
appears here. The only exception is the notification dot for unread messages,
which uses Ember Malt at 6px diameter — the minimum necessary to signal without
alarming.

6.6  PROFILE / QUESTS / REWARDS  ·  Season: Winter × Gold — Ritual
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The Profile section is the ritual zone. Night Sky (#1C1E2C) surfaces have a
quality of dark stone — not the cool blue-dark of the global system, not the
warm brown-dark of the store. Night Sky is neutral dark with a barely perceptible
blue undertone, grounding the section in solemnity.

The avatar glow uses Warm Platinum (#C8C4BE) — a deliberately non-gold choice.
Platinum signals identity and pack membership. Gold signals achievement. The
avatar is who you are; the level badge dot (in Fort Knox gold) is what you have
done. These two reads must remain distinct. Avatar border: 2px at 45% opacity.
Avatar glow: 0 0 20px at 18% opacity.

Quest progress uses the 11:11 motif: every progress indicator is two parallel
2px lines, 4px apart. This references the 11:11 single's ritual time-as-devotion
iconography. The lines fill left to right in Teal Green (#007A78) while active,
transitioning to a Fort Knox gold gradient on completion.

Reward coupons are styled as physical concert tickets — a dashed vertical
tear-line separates the information panel from the stub, which carries the
redemption code in rotated Barlow Condensed. Standard rewards use Oyster Gray
(#BCB6A2) as accent. Exclusive rewards use Fort Knox gold with a 0 0 24px glow.
Creole Pink (#EECCC0) appears only on the tear-line — a warm parchment-adjacent
color that makes the ticket feel tangible without literally mimicking paper.

7.  GLASSMORPHISM IDENTITY
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

The glassmorphism works in this system because the backgrounds are narrow in
saturation and rich in depth — Estate Blue, Navy Blazer, and Night Sky all have
enough tonal complexity to produce a meaningful blur without becoming muddy.

Standard glass recipe (system-wide):
  Background      rgba(31, 58, 92, 0.18)           Estate Blue at 18% opacity
  Tint            rgba(39, 168, 219, 0.06)          Suite Cyan trace, ambient warmth
  Backdrop filter blur(16px) saturate(140%)
  Top border      1px solid rgba(235, 238, 228, 0.08)   Cloud Dancer specular
  Side/btm border 1px solid rgba(235, 238, 228, 0.03)   barely present
  Noise overlay   SVG feTurbulence at 3–5% opacity       prevents banding
  Shadow          0 8px 32px rgba(0, 0, 0, 0.5)

Community section modification:
  Fill opacity increases to 80% (vs 60% standard)
  Backdrop blur decreases
  Result: walls, not windows — enclosed and comfortable

Checkout card modification:
  Specular top border increases to 12% opacity
  Result: the glass feels like a receipt holder at the moment of purchase


────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
8.  GRAPHIC MOTIFS
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Three graphic motifs recur across the system, each tied to a specific element
of ROA's visual world.

THE WINDOW
───────────
Drawn from Private Suite Vol. 2 album artwork — a house at night with windows
glowing red from within. In the app, the sold-out product card is its expression:
a Navy Blazer surface (cool, exterior) with desaturation overlay (blacked out).
The window is dark. The room is closed. The "CLOSED" label in Barlow Condensed
completes the metaphor without explaining it.

THE HALFTONE GRAIN
───────────────────
Drawn from BLOODY and NOON cover art references. An SVG feTurbulence noise
overlay at 14–18% opacity applies to the landing hero section only, giving the
digital surface the texture of an offset-printed poster. It ages the image
slightly — making the modern feel archival, consistent with ROA's blend of
contemporary trap production and medieval warrior imagery.

THE 11:11 TWIN LINES
─────────────────────
Drawn directly from ROA's 11:11 single, which uses time-as-ritual as a thematic
anchor. In the Profile section, every quest progress indicator is two parallel
2px lines rather than a single bar. The gap between them is exactly 4px — they
read as a pair, as symmetry, as the four vertical strokes of "11:11." This motif
should never be used outside the Profile / Quests context, where its meaning is
coherent.

9.  KEY DESIGN RULES — NON-NEGOTIABLE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

  Lava Falls        Appears on ONE element per full-page view. Not one per
                    section. One per view. If used twice, it loses all power.

  Fort Knox         Achievement color exclusively. Does not appear in navigation,
                    decoration, borders, or hover states. Ever.

  Warm Platinum     Identity color exclusively. Does not appear on quests,
                    rewards, or any earned-state UI. It marks who you are,
                    not what you have done.

  Space Mono        Required for all prices, countdown numerals, rank numbers,
                    and statistics. Proportional fonts are not permitted for
                    these values.

  Bebas Neue        Uppercase only. Never sentence case, never lowercase. One
                    weight, one style, no exceptions.

  11:11 Bars        Twin-line motif appears only in Profile / Quests. Do not
                    apply as decoration elsewhere.

  Creole Pink       Appears only on reward coupon tear-lines. Not a text color,
                    border color, or background tint in any other context.

  Glass Opacity     Community fill opacity: 80%. System-wide fill opacity: 60%.
                    These values are not interchangeable.

  Sold Out Card     Navy Blazer (#1A2038) + filter:saturate(0) + dark overlay.
                    The temperature shift from warm active cards is the primary
                    unavailability signal, not the badge or label alone.

  Hover States      Use only Teal Green (#009E9C) or the immediate lighter shade
                    of the current surface. No other color receives hover treatment.
